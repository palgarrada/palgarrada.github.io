I am a predoctoral researcher in the Department of Economic History at the University of Barcelona (UB), specializing in Business History with a focus on urban economic transformation and institutional entrepreneurship.

My research examines the dynamic interplay between economic development and evolving institutional frameworks, particularly in post-industrial contexts where historical legacies must be strategically repurposed for contemporary economic growth.

Research Interests

My main research interests center on the institutional pathways through which urban infrastructures – including historic city centres, facilities left after major events, and derelict industrial zones – are repurposed and adapted through new governance structures to foster local and regional economic growth.

I am particularly interested in analyzing how collaborative arrangements between public administrations, private sector actors, and academic institutions catalyze entrepreneurial dynamism and regional development, with special attention to emerging forms such as science parks and innovation districts.

Methodology & Approach

Methodologically, I employ a longitudinal case study approach that combines rigorous archival analysis of business and institutional sources with qualitative interviews to trace the evolutionary trajectories of these complex transformation processes. This historically-grounded perspective allows me to contribute to broader theoretical debates on path dependence, institutional change, and the role of entrepreneurial agency in economic restructuring, bridging business history with contemporary innovation and urban or management studies.
